Plastic Bottle

This item belongs in your curbside recycling bin after rinsing.

Learn More About Plastics arrow_forward

Step-by-Step Instructions

delete_outline

1. Empty Bottle

Make sure the bottle is completely empty of any liquid.

water_drop

2. Rinse Clean

Give the bottle a quick rinse with water to remove residue. No need to use soap.

refresh

3. Replace Cap

Screw the plastic cap back on the bottle. This helps ensure the cap gets recycled too.

recycling

4. Place in Bin

Put the prepared bottle in your curbside recycling bin. Do not bag it.

Do's

  • check_circle

    Recycle bottles from water, soda, and juice.

  • check_circle

    Leave the labels on; they are removed during processing.

  • check_circle

    Keep the cap on the bottle to ensure it's recycled.

Don'ts

  • cancel

    Do not flatten the bottles; 3D shapes are easier for sorting machines.

  • cancel

    Do not include bottles that held hazardous materials like motor oil.

  • cancel

    Do not place plastic bags in the recycling bin with your bottles.

Why It Matters

expand_more

Recycling one ton of plastic bottles saves 1.5 tons of carbon dioxide. It also conserves natural resources and energy, reduces landfill waste, and prevents pollution of our oceans and waterways.

Local Variations

expand_more

Recycling rules can vary by city and state. Some municipalities may require caps to be removed, while others accept a wider range of plastic types. Always check with your local waste management provider for the most accurate information.
